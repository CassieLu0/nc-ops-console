Analysis summary

Avg price per DSP

Bars = volume-weighted avg $/pkg for each DSP. Dashed line = selection average. Hover for volume served.

DSP × station price matrix

Simple avg $/pkg per DSP in each station where it operates (not volume-weighted); darker green = higher price. Network = station values weighted by the DSP's ZIP-share per station. ZIPs = priced ZIP count attributed to the DSP.
Method. Chart & KPIs: for each DSP, avg price = Σ(ZIP price × DSP volume in that ZIP) ÷ Σ(DSP volume), over priced ZIPs only. Matrix: volume is not used — each cell is the simple mean of the DSP's ZIP prices in that station; a multi-station DSP's Network value weights its station cells by the percentage of its ZIP footprint in each station (hover a cell to see ZIP count and % share); the Station avg row is the simple mean of all priced ZIPs in the station.
Price sheet. This page reads data/price_adjustments.csv the same way the map does — commit an updated CSV and these figures recompute on next load. The chip next to the selector shows whether adjustments are active.
